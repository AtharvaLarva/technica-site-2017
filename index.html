The All-Ladies Hackathon

November 7-8, 2015.
College Park, MD.

This is tech culture beyond hello world.

Technica is the University of Maryland’s first all-ladies hackathon. Over the duration of 24 hours, ladies are immersed in tech culture and encouraged to exercise their imagination to create interesting and innovative hacks. Technica is diverging from the typical hackathon as we encourage gender diversification, and ask sponsors to hack alongside students in order to build stronger connections between students, sponsors, and the tech world.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why make an all-ladies hackathon?

Diversification of the tech world in general is vital to its continuing success. By creating a more welcoming atmosphere, we hope to foster a greater connection between ladies and the tech world.

Do I need to know how to code to participate?

No, of course not! In fact, we encourage students that do not know coding to attend. The event is made to introduce you to tech culture. You can learn everything you need right here.

How do I register?

Registration opens August 15th at 11:59 pm EST and closes September 19th at 11:59 pm EST. Until registration opens, please stay tuned via email by signing up for our mailing list.

How will I get to College Park?

Technica will send buses to three major hubs based on popular registration areas. Furthermore, if you are traveling more than 30 miles to College Park, you will be reimbursed for your travel costs (up to a cap) contingent on you uploading your travel receipts prior to the weekend of the event. Note that we will only reimburse the least expensive travel route. Special bus arrangements will be made for high school students attending.

Do I have to pay to attend?

No! The event includes free admission, free food, free overnight stay, and a lot of free gear!

What should I bring?

You should bring a valid student ID for admission, and your laptop for hacking. We encourage attendees to bring a sleeping bag, a pillow, and personal toiletries. Everything else - food, drinks, air mattresses, and more - will be provided! Note that in order to borrow hardware during the hackathon, a government issued photo ID is required.

How do teams work?

Teams can be between 2 to 4 people. You can register your team on our website at any point before the 24 hour hack starts. Note this is not the same as personal registration - please register yourself individually first.

Can I come without having a preset team?

Of course! If you don’t already have a team, don’t worry about it. We will designate time for team formation and project brainstorming before the official hacking begins.
